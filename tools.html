ESSENTIAL TOOLS NEEDED TO PREPARE A HERBARIUM SPECIMEN

Tips

  1. Whenever feasible, gather the complete plant, including the roots, stems, leaves, flowers, fruits, and seeds, as these are frequently required for precise identification.
  2. To avoid wilting or loss of structure, press the plant as soon as possible after collecting.
  3. To avoid hurting delicate plant parts, such as little blooms or fine hairs, use tweezers or fine brushes when mounting.




Introduction to Herbarium Tools

Appropriate use of tools are important in assuring that the plants are adequately conserved for scientific research. The tools employed in this technique contribute to the specimen's integrity by conserving vital elements such as leaves, blooms, stems, and roots for proper identification and long-term storage. In this guide, we'll look at the fundamental instruments for creating a high-quality herbarium specimen that can persist for decades, if not centuries.

DRAFT

Trowel

A trowel is useful while creating a herbarium specimen since it allows you to delicately take plants, particularly their roots, from the ground without inflicting too much damage. It allows you to gently dig out the entire plant, preserving important distinguishing elements like root structure for scientific documentation and research.

DRAFT

Plant Press

A plant press is used for preparing a herbarium specimen because it flattens and dries the plant, retaining its shape and vital characteristics for scientific research and long-term storage. It ensures that the plant dries properly without decomposing, making it easier to identify and preserve as part of a collection.

DRAFT

Parchment Papers

Protects sensitive plant parts during the pressing process. It keeps the plant from clinging to the absorbent paper, making it easier to handle and remove after drying. Furthermore, parchment paper helps to keep the specimen's shape and detail, ensuring that fine portions such as petals and leaves remain intact for thorough research and long-term preservation.

DRAFT

Plastic foil

Protects the specimens from dampness, and the pressure from the belts maintains the specimens flat and in good contact with the paper as the mounting glue cures.

DRAFT

Acid-free adhesive

Keeps the plant and paper from degrading over time, maintaining the specimen's integrity and preservation for long-term study and exhibition.
