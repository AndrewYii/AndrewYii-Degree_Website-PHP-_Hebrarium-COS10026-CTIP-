TUTORIAL ON HOW TO TRANSFER A LEAF INTO A HERBARIUM SPECIES

Tips

  1. When collecting, look for healthy, undamaged plants that accurately represent the species.
  2. Ensure that pieces such as leaves, flowers, or stems do not overlap excessively during pressing, so that all critical aspects are visible for study.
  3. Label each specimen in the field with a collection number or code, and match this with detailed notes in a field notebook to avoid confusion during mounting and cataloging




Introduction to Herbarium Making

In this tutorial, we will walk you through the step-by-step process of properly preserving fresh leaves into herbarium specimens. Whether you are new to plant preservation or looking to improve your techniques, this guide will provide you with all the necessary tools and best practices for maintaining the integrity of your leaf samples.

DRAFT

Step 1: Collecting

When looking for specimens to add to your herbarium, the best places to look are along edges, where you will find the most diversity. Search along pathways, and field and wooland borders. Fence lines and railways also have a great variety, as the plants are protected from the frequent trimming other areas are subjected to.

DRAFT

Step 2: Preparation

Post collection, protect the specimens from damage during transport. It is high suggested to place them in sealable plastic bags within a leather satchel to help moisture and secure them from both heat and breakage.

DRAFT

Step 3: Pressing

After proper preparation, load the plants into the press, making sure there is a layer of parchment between the plants and the plywood dividers on both top and bottom. After the press is full, apply even pressure to keep the plants flat while they dry.

DRAFT

Step 4: Mounting

Once the plants dry off, they are ready to be mounted. It is crucial to use acid-free paper and adhesive to help ensure preservation of the specimens. You could use an acrylic adhesive that allows flexibility after it dries, which would accommodate for the small amount of expansion and contraction that the plant will go through when in storage.

DRAFT

Step 5: Freezing

After mounting the specimens, place each one between layers of parchment paper, separated by plywood dividers, just as they were during pressing. The stack is then topped with thick boards on both sides, wrapped in plastic foil, and tightly secured with adjustable straps.
