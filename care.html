CARE: PRESERVING THE HERBARIUM

Quick Tips for Long-Term Preservation

  1. Store specimens in a cool, dry place to avoid mold and moisture.
  2. Avoid direct sunlight to prevent fading.
  3. Regularly check for pests and use natural repellents.
  4. Handle specimens gently with gloves to avoid oils damaging the paper.
  5. Ensure proper labeling to maintain scientific accuracy.

Introduction to Herbarium Prevention

Plant specimens are stored for herbarium preservation in order to keep them undamaged and useful for future generations. Botanists, researchers, and enthusiasts can study plant diversity, identify species, and even analyze changes in plant populations over time thanks to specimens that have been properly maintained. These guidelines will help you produce an enduring, aesthetically pleasing, and scientifically valuable record of plant life.

Step 1: Assign and Record Information

Every plant has a special identifying number allocated to it. This number is connected to a digital database that stores related images and comprehensive plant data for later use.

Step 2: Protect the Specimens

Place each dried herbarium specimen inside a clear, acid-free plastic or paper sleeve to prevent damage. This shields the specimen from light and physical harm, preserving its quality throughout time.

Step 3: Label the Specimen

Each sleeve is labeled with key information, including the identification number, the botanical and common names of the plant, and details like the place and date of collection.

Step 4: Store the Specimens

The finished, labeled specimens are kept in wooden boxes that are intended to shield them from light and other elements. In order to help keep insect pests away, cedar shavings are placed to the cases.

Step 5: Inspect and Maintain the Collection

Regularly check the specimens in storage for evidence of insect damage. In order to eradicate pests and safeguard the collection, it could be necessary to freeze the specimens if damage is found.

Acid-Free Paper
A type of paper that resists deterioration and is used for long-term storage of delicate materials, such as plant specimens.
Pests
Insects or organisms that can damage stored herbarium specimens by feeding on the plant material.
